Notetaking apps

Dropbox Paper¶

Dropbox Paper is a sleek, minimal text editor that keeps all your docs in the cloud dropbox, no matter where you are right now. Dropbox Paper lets work on documents with other people simultaneously and easily add multimedia elements to documents (like videos or Google docs). It's available for Android Phones free, iPhones free and Google Chrome free.

Evernote¶

Evernote lets you take notes, create to-do lists, and save things you find online. It also allows you to include a copy of whole websites (Evernote Web Clipper) within your notes and syncs everything between your phone, tablet, and computer automatically. It's available for Android Phones free, iPhones free and Google Chrome free.

Google Keep¶

Google Keep allows you to make different kinds of notes, including text, lists, images, and audio. You can set time or even location-based reminders. Voice recordings created through Google Keep are automatically transcribed. It's available for Android Phones free, iPhones free and Google Chrome free.

Google Apps¶

Google Apps is a bundle of productivity tolls for your online studies. The most important ones are Google Docs , Google Sheets and Google Slides .

Google Docs is an online editor that can be used for note taking.
Google Sheets is an online spreadsheet tool that can be used to run some basic calculations.
Google Slides is an online presentation tool that can be used to prepare presentation slides for assignments or for sharing them with fellow students.

Google Docs is available for Android Phones free, iPhones free and Google Chrome free. Google Sheets is available for for Android Phones free, iPhones free and Google Chrome free. Google Slides is available for for Android Phones free, iPhones free and Google Chrome free.

OneNote gathers users' notes (handwritten or typed), drawings, screen clippings and audio commentaries. It also allows you to include a copy of whole websites OneNote within your notes and syncs everything between your phone, tablet, and computer automatically. It's available for Android Phones free, iPhones free and Google Chrome free.

Todoist¶

Todoist is a project management application for personal productivity. It allows you to manage your tasks from a smartphone, tablet or computer. It's available for Android Phones free, iPhones free and Google Chrome free.

Wunderlist¶

Wunderlist allows you to create lists to manage your tasks on nearly any device. Through the "Detail View" you can add due dates (including repeating due dates), reminders, assignees, subtasks, comments, files and notes to tasks. It's available for Android Phones free, iPhones free and Google Chrome free.
